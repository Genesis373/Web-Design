About Black Rhinos

Black rhinos are members of Afirca's Big Five, which are the most difficult and dangerous African animals to hunt on foot. Countries where most of them are from are Kenya, Namibia, Zimbabwe, and South Africa. Most rhinos live on wildlife-conservation reserves and can live in many different habitats, such as savannahs, woodlands, forests, and wetlands.

Due to living in environments with high temperatures, black rhinos will limit their movement to stay comfortable They can also survive in areas where flowing water is scarce or nonexistent. They frequently drink water where it is available and will sometimes dig for it in dry riverbeds. sometimes to protect themselves from bugs and the hot savannah sun, black rhinos will often take mud baths to cool off and protect themselves, just like an elephant.

While the male of the species mostly keeps to himself, females will sometimes stay together for long periods of time. Male black rhinos will start mating and claiming territory around 10-12 years of age. Oftentimes, males will get into conflits with each other over a female during courtship, which may result in the death of one of them. Black rhino breeding isn't seasonal and occurs year-round. Calves are born to a family at an interval of 2.5-3.5 years.
