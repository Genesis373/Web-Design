Black Rhinoceros

Your Guide to the Dino-like Mammals

What are Black Rhinos?

Rhinoceroses are type of mammal that is divided into two species: the black rhino and the white rhino. What sets these two species apart, ironically, has nothing to do with their colors. Black rhinos are the smallest rhino while white rhinos are the largest. While white rhinos feast on grass for food, black rhinos have a diet that mainly consists of leaves. Black rhinos mostly live in solitude while white rhinos stick to groups of up to 10 animals. The most well-known feature that black rhinos have is their pointed upper lip, which is why they are often called "hook-lipped rhinos".

Whil black rhinos are small than white rhinos, they can still grow to be 1.5 meters high and 3.5 meters long. Both males and females weigh 1500 and 2900 pounds. They also have two horns with the front one being the longest of the two, measuring around 50cm long. They also have a particular liking for acacia trees, possibly because they produce leaves and twigs that are an essential to a black rhino's diet.

Black rhinoceroses have very bad eyesight, but they make up for it with their excellent senses of hearing and smell. They are most often ill-tempered and quite unpredictable, and if they hear or smell anything that is unfamiliar to them, tehy will charge straight for it. They are also very strong and very fast, and and can run at speeds up to 30 miles per hour, and despite their huge size and weight, they are very agile and can make quick U-turns if they miss a target. Black rhinos can also commununicate with each other through infrasonic frequencies, which are soundwaves that are made at a decible level that humans cannot hear.
