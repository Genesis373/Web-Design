Black Rhinoceros

Creature Interactions

While they are mostly solitary animals, black rhinos do live in peace with some animals. One animal that in almost always seen on them are oxpeckers, also known as tickbirds. Since black rhinos are very large creatures, they are prone to having ticks and bugs feasting on their backs. That's where oxpeckers come in; these birds perch on the backs of these large creatures and will eat the pests off of their backs. Each animal wins in this symbiotic relationship; the oxpeckers have a source of food, and the black rhinos have a clean and uninfested back.

Wildebeests are large, hairy, buffalo-like animals that live in Africa. While they don't have a pairing relationship with black rhinos like the oxpecker does, they do have a mutual understanding between each other. Wildebeests will often signal the horned animals of any approaching predators; in turn, black rhinos will protect ox-like creatures from any predators attacking them.

Lions are predators of black rhinos, alongside humans and hyenas. The king of the beasts will oftentimes hunt them as a source of food for their pride. They have excellent hunting skills, and due to black rhinos having poor eyesight, they can pounce without warning. While this does prove dangerous for a black rhino, their thick skin acts like a suit of armor, their agility helps them shake a lion off its back, and sometimes, it can even attack the king of the beast if it gets onto its territory.
