Jewel Properties

The true nature of a jewel comes from how it interacts with light. Light is what makes a gemstone beautiful and desirable and is the source of its beauty, color, and sparkle. It can also help identify different types of gems, such as determining a spinel from a ruby or tourmaline from emeralds. These jewels, as well as others, may seem to have the same color, but they each feature different hues that give off different shades. Each jewel type has a unique set of optical properties, which just goes to show that no two gems are the same. Light, however, is just ones way to identify jewel types.

There are other properties that can set jewel types off from one another. Color is one of the most important parts about the desirable qualities of a jewel. Some colors can be seen as more desirable, some are used to show social status, and some have even connected some gems to astrology and mysticism. Another factor is a gems luster, which is how a gem surface reacts with reflected light. The quality of a jewel is one of the most important properties for jewelry. While some gems have more internal flaws than others, they could all still be equally desirable.
